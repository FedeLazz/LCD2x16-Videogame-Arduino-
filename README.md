I wondered how you can use a 16x2 LCD screen to create a videogame? I realized that the best way would have been to use Line 0 (top) of the LCD as a HUD, where it shows text related to game actions, such as an item obtained, or the location (such as a map that says the section/room you are in), a counter of keys used to open doors, and a special space to show on the screen if the player is in possession of at least 1 bomb, It can be used to open doors in the absence of keys or to momentarily defeat the enemy that will haunt us during the game.
And used Line 1 (bottom) for the actual game, objects, environment, enemies etc
Game:

The game involves exploring floors and corridors; each floor contains several corridors.
The player can interact with crates to obtain keys used to open doors, allowing access to new floors or secret rooms, or to obtain bombs that can be used to open doors or defeat enemies.

The player can also hide in lockers/wardrobes where, if they are being chased by an enemy, the enemy will lose sight of them and stop the pursuit, returning to a non-chasing state.
